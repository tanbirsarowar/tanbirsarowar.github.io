About

"I build instruments that see what no eye can: the hidden air gaps, stress fields, and conformity failures between a silicon wafer and the vacuum chuck that holds it during chip fabrication."

I am a PhD candidate in the Precision Metrology & Instrumentation (PMI) Group at Texas A&M University, advised by Prof. ChaBum Lee. My research addresses a critical unsolved problem in advanced semiconductor manufacturing: how does a 300 mm silicon wafer actually conform to a vacuum chuck, and how do nanometer-scale surface imperfections propagate into overlay and CD errors at the 3 nm technology node?

I tackle this through a multi-pronged approach developing new optical and impedance-based metrology tools, building physics-based fluid-structure interaction simulations, and translating findings toward real-time in-line inspection. I also bring direct industry perspective from my internship at ASML, where I worked on High-NA EUV machine commissioning at Intel's D1X fab.

Before TAMU, I completed my MS at Washington State University Vancouver, applying machine learning to microfluidic device design for cancer cell separation. My undergraduate work at the Islamic University of Technology (Bangladesh) focused on thermal management with nano-liquid-metal coolants.

Wafer–Chuck Interface Metrology

Developing a suite of non-destructive sensing and simulation tools to characterize the microscale air gap between 300 mm silicon wafers and vacuum chucks — directly impacting overlay accuracy in advanced semiconductor lithography.

Project 01 · EIS Metrology

Electrochemical Impedance Spectroscopy for Wafer-Chuck Interface Characterization

Pioneered the use of EIS as a spatial metrology tool for the wafer-chuck interface. A ball-ended magnetic probe scans the wafer surface while the nickel-plated ceramic chuck acts as the bottom electrode, forming a capacitive stack: probe → wafer → air gap → chuck. The Nyquist diagram maps spatial impedance across the wafer, fingerprinting air gap magnitude, chuck damage, and vacuum state.

Z_gap = 1/(jωC_gap),   C_gap = ε₀·A / h(r)
Series model: Z_total = Z_probe + Z_wafer + Z_gap + Z_chuck
Spacer validation: +25 MΩ per 50 µm gap layer

Project 02 · Optical Sensing

NIR Fabry-Pérot Reflection Spectroscopy for Air Gap Measurement

Developed a 1064 nm NIR reflection spectroscopy system that exploits silicon's transparency in the near-infrared to directly measure the air gap between the wafer's bottom surface and the chuck top. The gap forms a Fabry-Pérot cavity whose interference fringes encode thickness — extracted via FFT of the reflection spectrum.

FP condition: 2·n_air·h = m·λ  →  h = m·λ / (2·Δf·n_air)
FFT peak frequency → gap thickness h(r)
Resolution: Δh ≈ λ²/(2·Δλ) → micrometer-scale sensitivity
Cross-validated with Lion Precision CPL190 (2.8 mV/µm)

Project 03 · Computational Physics

Multi-physics FSI Simulation Suite for Transient Wafer Chucking

Built a complete fluid-structure interaction (FSI) simulation suite in Python from scratch — coupling compressible Reynolds lubrication theory with Kirchhoff-Love plate mechanics to predict the transient conformity of a 300 mm wafer on grooved and porous vacuum chucks. Both 1D (radially symmetric) and full 2D solvers implemented with Newmark-β time integration.

Reynolds (compressible): ∂/∂r[r·h³·∂p/∂r] = 12µ·∂(ph)/∂t
Plate bending: D·∇⁴w = q(r,t),   D = Eh³/[12(1−ν²)]
Gap dynamics: h(r,t) = h₀(r) − w(r,t)
Contact: Greenwood-Williamson (GW) asperity model
Slip-flow: Fukui-Kaneko Kn correction for nanoscale gaps

Project 06 · Wafer Bonding Inspection

Non-Destructive Bonded Wafer Quality Assessment by Electrochemical Impedance Spectroscopy

Extended the EIS metrology framework from the wafer-chuck interface to the inspection of hybrid-bonded wafer stacks — a critical need as 3D IC packaging (SiCN-SiCN, Cu-Cu direct bonding) becomes the dominant interconnect technology. Samsung-provided SiCN fusion-bonded 300 mm wafer pairs were mapped using a 37-point polar grid and 7×7 grid, revealing spatial bonding quality with sub-MΩ sensitivity. Presented at the Texas Semiconductor Manufacturers (TSM) Annual Meeting 2026.

Equivalent circuit: Z = Z_probe + Z_wafer + Z_bond + Z_substrate
Bonding layer: modeled as parallel R_bond ∥ C_bond (CPE element)
Well-bonded: R_bond ≈ 622 kΩ (matched to theory)
Defective / void region: R_bond → 2–3 MΩ (impedance rise)
Post-anneal uniformity: σ reduces from 1.18 MΩ → 0.10 MΩ

Masters Thesis · WSU Vancouver · 2023–2024

ML-Guided Design of DLD Microfluidic Devices for Cancer Cell Separation

Developed a data-driven design framework for Deterministic Lateral Displacement (DLD) microfluidic devices targeting circulating tumor cell (CTC) separation from blood. Combined high-fidelity COMSOL CFD simulations with supervised machine learning to create a fast surrogate model replacing hours of CFD with millisecond predictions.

DLD critical diameter: D_c = f(N_pillars, D_pillar, Gap, λ)
Dataset: 1,046 device configs × 8,100 particles = 8.5M trajectories
Random Forest: R² = 0.994, MAE = 0.31 µm
Speedup: >10,000× over iterative CFD design

  • Compared 4 ML algorithms: Random Forest outperforms GB, KNN, and MLP

Undergraduate Thesis · IUT · 2019–2021

Microchannel Heat Sink Performance with Nano-Liquid-Metal-Fluid Coolants

Numerically investigated advanced microchannel heat sink designs using nano-liquid-metal-fluid (nano-LMF) coolants for high-performance electronic cooling. Used ANSYS FLUENT for conjugate heat transfer simulations, parametrically comparing multiple coolant compositions and ceramic substrate materials to identify optimal thermal resistance configurations.

  • Nano-liquid-metal fluids show significantly higher heat removal capacity than conventional coolants
  • Five ceramic substrate materials compared: optimal thermal-mechanical combination identified
  • Boron-based ceramic materials outperform standard substrates in combined thermal-structural figure of merit
  • Published 3 journal papers (Elsevier Ceramics International, ASME J. Thermal Science, Adv. Mater. Res.) as first/co-author
